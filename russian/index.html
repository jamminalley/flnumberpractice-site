The big picture

Russian numbers are compositional, written as separate words. The lexical surprises are minor — сорок (40) and девяносто (90) are the two outliers. The real difficulty is the case agreement: the noun after a number changes form depending on whether the number ends in 1, 2-4, or anything else.

i
If you only remember one thing: the number doesn't change much, but the noun after it does. Один рубль, два рубля, пять рублей — all "rubles," all spelled differently because the number is doing grammar.

05

Case agreement — the killer rule

The noun after a number takes a different case depending on the number's last digit. Three buckets. This is what makes Russian numbers feel grammatical rather than lexical — and it applies to every noun.

1 Nominative singular. The dictionary form. Один рубль. Also: 21, 31, 41, 101… … 1, 21, 31
2 – 4 Genitive singular. "Of one ruble," used three times. Два рубля, три рубля, четыре рубля. Also: 22-24, 32-34, etc. … 2, 22, 33, 44
5+ Genitive plural. "Of rubles." Пять рублей, десять рублей, сто рублей. Includes all teens 11-19, regardless of their last digit. … 5-20, 25, 99
!
The teens always go to bucket 3. 11, 12, 13, 14 use the 5+ rule (gen plur), not their last-digit rule. Одиннадцать рублей, not одиннадцать рубль. Двенадцать рублей, not двенадцать рубля. The "last digit" rule only kicks in once you're past the teens.
i
Years use a different form. "I'm 25 years old" = Мне 25 лет ("twenty-five years"), where лет is the special genitive plural form — not годов. Dates use neuter ordinals in genitive: 2022 год = две тысячи двадцать второго года.
06

Things to remember

Five rules that will save you from the most common mistakes.

1.
The noun's case follows the number. Один рубль, два рубля, пять рублей — three forms for "rubles," chosen by the number. Teens always count as 5+.
2.
Two tens are outliers: сорок (40) and девяносто (90). The other tens fit predictable patterns (-дцать or -десят). Just memorize these two.
3.
Gender on 1 and 2 only. Один / одна / одно, два / две. The other digits don't inflect for gender.
4.
Hundreds aren't predictable. 200 = двести, 300 = триста, 500 = пятьсот. Three subpatterns (-сти, -ста, -сот) across the nine forms.
5.
Big numbers are separate words. No fusion (like German), no "and" (like Spanish). 1.348 = тысяча триста сорок восемь. Four words, four building blocks.
